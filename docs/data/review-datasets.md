# Review Datasets

This document describes the collections of reviews used in the project and the annotations applied to them.

## Review sets

### Train split (2,700 reviews)

90% of the original 3,000-review train CSV (`NNmodels/data/yelp_review_train.csv`), split using `random_state=42`. These are the reviews the DistilBERT classifier was trained on. The original train CSV is balanced: 1,500 positive, 1,500 negative.

The train/dev split happens at data-loading time — both splits come from the same file. Includes restaurant metadata (name, city, state, stars, categories), review text, and review stars.

### Dev split (300 reviews)

The remaining 10% of the train CSV, used as a validation set during training. "Dev" (short for "development") and "validation" are interchangeable terms in machine learning. Same columns as train.

### Test split (3,000 reviews)

A separate held-out set (`NNmodels/data/yelp_review_test.csv`) that the model never saw during training. Balanced: 1,500 positive, 1,500 negative. Same columns as train.

### Synthetic GPT reviews (432 reviews)

Generated by GPT-4o via `NNmodels/code/gen_synth_data.py`. A balanced factorial design: 6 cuisines (Japanese, Indian, Mexican, Italian, American, Thai) × 3 locations (urban, suburban, rural) × 2 food qualities (wonderful, terrible) × 2 service qualities (wonderful, terrible) × 2 ambience qualities (wonderful, terrible) = 144 combinations, 3 reviews each.

These are entirely artificial — not derived from Yelp data. Source file: `NNmodels/data/synthetic_gpt_reviews.csv`.

## Annotations

### Sentiment label

Present on all Yelp reviews as the `rating` column: positive or negative. Derived from star ratings — 4-5 stars = positive, 1-2 stars = negative. There are no 3-star reviews in the dataset.

### GPT quality ratings

Food, service, and ambience each rated as wonderful (1), neutral (0), or terrible (-1). Applied to 1,842 of the 3,000 test reviews — the subset with a valid ZIP code (mappable to a RUCA urban/suburban/rural classification) AND American, Italian, or Mexican in the restaurant categories. Rated by GPT-4o with `temperature=0`.

Source file: `NNmodels/data/yelp_review_test_gpt_rated.csv`.

These 1,842 reviews are the same ones that have pre-computed SHAP data and form the basis of `explorer_data_with_words.json`. Whether this overlap is by design or coincidence is unclear, but the counts match exactly.

For the synthetic reviews, quality attributes (wonderful/terrible only, no neutral) are generation parameters, not post-hoc annotations — they were the inputs to GPT-4o when creating the review text.

### DistilBERT model predictions

The trained DistilBERT classifier's predictions are saved for two review sets:

- **Test reviews** (3,000): `NNmodels/distilbert_trained_results/yelp_test_attributes_simple.csv` includes `predict_positive` (0 or 1) and `predict_probability` (confidence score) columns alongside the review attributes.
- **Synthetic GPT reviews** (432): `NNmodels/data/synthetic_gpt_reviews_and_labels.csv` adds a `rating` column (positive/negative) to the synthetic reviews. Since these reviews are artificial, this prediction is the only sentiment label they have.

Predictions on the dev and train splits are computed during training but not saved to files.
